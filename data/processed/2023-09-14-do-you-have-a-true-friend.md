**Marks of a true friend**

Friendship is such a fascinating phenomenon.   
Two random strangers meet, connect, and start to share a bond.   
A bond that takes them through an adventure.   
Often joyous, sometimes painful, occasionally devastating.   
But irrespective of our experience, the notion of friendship rarely changes.

We continue to make and break friendships.

So, what makes for a fruitful friendship?   
One that can last longer, one that will stand the test of time?

Here are 3 signs that I think qualify a true friend (and thus friendship):

1. **No one is truly your friend until they have seen the worst of you and still choose to stay.  
​**[(Share on x)](https://ctt.ac/buL37)​  
It is easy to be a friend when things are lovely, happy, and joyful.   
When it is all about partying, laughing and watching movies together, drinking and dining together, the trips together.   
​  
But each of us has a dark side.   
A side that we are perhaps scared of ourselves. Embarrassed. Guilty of.   
And one day, that side emerges in front of our friend.   
They are caught by surprise.   
And most frankly, at that point, they will leave.   
"I don't want to do anything with this", will be their reaction.   
​  
But that true friend - they will witness you at your worst and still choose to stay.   
Because they believe that that is not you.   
Because they believe you would want to change, would want to work on yourself, would want to become better.   
And they are willing to help.   
Because they care!   
​

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oaRF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa104d9f3-fb3e-43e5-935e-96199dbb722a_1024x1024.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oaRF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa104d9f3-fb3e-43e5-935e-96199dbb722a_1024x1024.jpeg)

​

2. **No one is truly your friend until they say "I believe in you" when you struggle to believe in yourself.  
​**[(Share on X)](https://ctt.ac/c24Qq)​  
Remember those moments when things were hard? Times were hard? Everything felt hard?   
When you felt you would not make it, when you felt ugly, helpless, stuck, incapable, inadequate, unwanted?   
When you felt that this was your life, forever!   
​  
At that moment, most people would leave you to it.   
"Can't handle a pessimist/toxic person such as this, who just brings negativity to my life", is what they would say.   
​  
But that one true friend would stare you in your eyes and say, "I believe in you."  
And sometimes all we need in our lives is for someone to say that, when we struggle to believe in our own self.   
​

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2zc_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffaa42042-cba1-4965-8ab2-99402e5ab171_1024x1024.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2zc_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffaa42042-cba1-4965-8ab2-99402e5ab171_1024x1024.jpeg)

​

3. **No one is truly your friend until they choose to tell you the truth, even though they might be affected personally for saying so.  
​**[(Share on X)](https://ctt.ac/hHzNA)​  
It is so easy to say what you want to hear.   
It is so hard to say what you should hear!   
A true friend will do that. Always. Because they know you deserve the truth.   
Knowing that you might feel hurt, might get angry, might feel blamed in that moment - they will still tell the truth.   
​  
And if any day your friend stops to tell you the truth, stops correcting your mistakes, stops saying what needs to be said - congrats.   
You have lost that friend!

Friends are special.   
Make sure the most special of them, get the most special of you.

# 📕 Book I'm reading this week

Finished reading [Several Short Sentences About Writing](https://amzn.to/44EmEm4), by Verlyn Klinkenborg. It is a writing book for readers. Do not pick it up if you do not read - you will not get anything from it. For those who are active readers and would love to write - pick this up. Brilliant read. Just my style of writing.

Started reading **"[Silence the Mind](https://amzn.to/3EDKlQC)" by Dr. Ramesh Manocha**, who is a global expert in meditation. The book is quite a lovely and simple read on the benefits of meditation and has a practical toolkit on how to get started. This is a gift from Ruchi's aunt and I am quite enjoying the book. If you are serious about meditation - pick this up!

1. THANKFULLY, the share of people who invest increases with age. It is still worrying that in the 20s only 75% and in the 30s only 89% are investing. Why not 95%? (Because most of them do not know how to.)
2. Even for the ones investing, the 20s and 30s are a confusing decade where most folks do not know if they are doing the right thing. Investing seems like a big deal!
3. As age increases, more and more people rely on experts to invest their money - that's smart. Even if you buy a mutual fund, you are essentially doing that - someone is managing that fund for you.

**My response:**​  
I invest and I know I am doing it right.   
That wasn't the case always. While I have been investing since I was 24, I did it mostly wrong until 35 and then corrected myself in the last decade.   
Next year, my 3rd book, "Make Epic Money", will try and solve this problem for everyone in their 20s, 30s and beyond!
