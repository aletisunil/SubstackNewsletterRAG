# Read this with an open mind

Last week’s newsletter touched a nerve.  
The subject line was: **“Papa passed away. I had to let you know.”**

Several of you wrote back saying it felt like clickbait. Some said it triggered them.

Instead of brushing it aside, I spent time replying and trying to understand what actually bothered people.

And as always, I turned the experience into a life lesson.

First, the data.

The assumption was that I used the subject line to spike opens.  
That didn’t happen.

78K people opened the email.  
Which is almost exactly the same as the past two months.

January weighted average views: 77.1K

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WQtR!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa03a3ef1-dfbd-4810-85e3-1fb90b71f303_1354x1052.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WQtR!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa03a3ef1-dfbd-4810-85e3-1fb90b71f303_1354x1052.png)

February weighted average views: 76.8K

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MkT4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc7a47784-a53a-4298-bbfb-c9126bea9c6b_1327x869.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MkT4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc7a47784-a53a-4298-bbfb-c9126bea9c6b_1327x869.png)

Second, the emotion

**The first group** simply read the story for what it was: a story about how AI is becoming a support system for people in difficult moments.  
Some shared similar experiences. Some said it moved them. Then they moved on.

**The second group** had lost a parent.  
They said the subject line triggered them.

I get that.  
Actually, I don’t fully get it.   
I haven’t lost a parent yet.

But what puzzled me was this:  
Many said they felt *even more triggered* when they realized it wasn’t my father who had passed away.

Someone’s father had died.  
That part wasn’t the problem.

The problem was that it wasn’t mine.

I asked a few of them:  
*“If it was my father who had passed away, would you feel triggered or sorry?”*Most said they would feel sorry.

Which means the trigger wasn’t the death.  
It was who the death belonged to.  
Think about that.

**The third group** was the most fascinating.

They thought my father had died.  
Then realized it was someone else’s father.  
And said: “We expected better from you.”

To them, here’s the lesson.

When you hear something terrible, and later discover it isn’t true, you have three choices.

**Choice 1:**  
*Thank God it’s not true.*

**Choice 2:**  
*What is this trying to tell me?*

**Choice 3:**  
*How dare you say such a thing?*

Choice 1 is beautiful.  
Imagine replying: *“My heart skipped a beat reading that. Glad your father is okay. Powerful story though.”*

Choice 2 is mature.  
Several people said the subject caught their attention, but the story moved them more.

Choice 3 is amusing.

Because you don’t control what people say.  
But you allow what they say to control how you feel.

And that’s a dangerous way to live.

People who get triggered by traffic, a bad dish at a restaurant, slow internet, a crashing laptop, or sudden rain… are people who still have a long way to go in controlling their life.

Because anything small can ruin their day.  
And that’s not the kind of life you want to live, is it?

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gHeF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3453fc66-5097-43a6-96e3-8205c9b8da36_1034x315.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gHeF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3453fc66-5097-43a6-96e3-8205c9b8da36_1034x315.png)

But the notion of breakfast, lunch and dinner became such a strong part of the consumer economy, that we feel humans need 3 meals to survive.   
We actually don’t :))
