**​  
Why do we love vacations?**

We all seem to love vacations.   
We look forward to it when we know they are upcoming.   
Feel terrible when it is about to end and miss it once we are back.

What about vacations make us go through these emotions?

Here is what I feel:

It is on a vacation that we feel in control.   
We feel we have control over our time, over when we get up, and when we sleep.   
We feel we have control over what we do, how we do it, and we are not mandated or ordered to operate a certain way.

Which is why, if we ever go on a vacation with someone who controls our time, we feel like it is the worst vacation ever!

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Liry!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbb007b98-1b7a-4c3a-9fc2-1f6ee7fdab36_720x720.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Liry!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbb007b98-1b7a-4c3a-9fc2-1f6ee7fdab36_720x720.jpeg)

​

It is on a vacation that we escape our reality.   
For a moment, we let go of the toxic office, our toxic relationships, the stress in our lives, and the lack of accomplishment.   
This is why if we ever go on a vacation with work on our mind or with the same people that we want to run away from, we feel like it was not a vacation at all!

It is on a vacation that we get a sense of progress.   
Checking off destinations, visiting all the planned spots, playing those games, reading those books, and sleeping those hours.   
This is why if we ever go on a vacation where there is no agenda, we tend to feel just as tired as we were when we went on the vacation.

This sense of control, this sense of escaping our reality, and this sense of progress - comprises the feeling of a vacation.

Which is why, if you feel these things in your life already — if you always have a sense of control, if you do not need to escape your reality, and if you know you are always progressing, you will rarely feel the need to go on a vacation!

A vacation, for most of us, is an escape, from our lives.   
Which will always result in us feeling “Why is this getting over?”   
When the right feeling should be “I can’t wait to go back to my life.”

***Instead of wondering when your next vacation is, maybe you should set up a life you don’t need to escape from.*** ​  
​**- Seth Godin**​

**Results of last week's survey**

Last week, I asked:

Do you believe that the first thing you do on 1st Jan, is the thing you tend to do the entire year?

* Yes, I believe in that because it works
* Yes, I believe in that but don't know if it works
* No, do not believe in it because it doesn't work
* No, do not believe in it
* Don't know

Here are the results:

Observations:

* Not surprisingly, most do not believe in it.   
  What's interesting, though, is the increasing percentage of "I believe in it because it works" as age increases. My explanation is that with age, people realise that the first thing you do sets the rhythm for the rest of the year.

My answer?  
I believe in it, but don't know if it works :)))
