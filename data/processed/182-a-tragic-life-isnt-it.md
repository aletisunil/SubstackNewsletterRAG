# I wish…

**Received an email this morning from Neha (name changed).**  
  
”I am a biologist by profession.  
**I moved to Germany when I was 23 for higher studies.**   
My parents, now in their mid-70s, miss me every day.   
I am their only child.   
  
Biology and biotechnology were my passions. I came to Germany with a lot of dreams. I dreamt of being a great scientist, only to lose my passion for the field during my Master’s Thesis.   
I worked post my studies in various Biotech firms.   
  
**Being a foreigner is not easy at all.**   
I learnt German when I was in Bangalore, and it was the language skill that kept me an attractive candidate in the job market.   
  
I faced several bad workplaces, being overworked, underpaid, underappreciated, mentally harassed, and even bore the physical brunt.   
  
A change happened when my mother was diagnosed with cancer in 2023.   
I was in Bangalore for my vacation when I got to know.   
I had to extend my vacation by a week.   
It was from my own annual leave.   
When I returned to Germany after the most painful month of my existence and even my mom's, **I was fired from my company for the extended vacation.**   
Funnily enough, the company was working on cancer immunotherapy.   
The irony.   
  
However, this stressful time changed my life in many ways.   
  
After 12 years of living here, I am still underpaid, earning way less than my peers with the same experience but in different fields.   
  
**I feel like a failure as I am unable to even reach the first step of my childhood dreams.**   
I live almost hand-to-mouth.   
No matter how skilled I am, I never seem to go ahead in life.   
**I am disappointed in myself and the field.**   
It is utterly disappointing that hard work has borne no fruit in my case, and I don't feel successful at all.  
  
Just sharing my thoughts and hope to hear yours.”  
  
**Here was my exact reply:**  
  
”I want you to imagine your death, Neha.  
You are in your bed, tucked in, warm and cosy.   
Surrounded by people you love.  
You know your time is up.  
You are 80 - you have lived a long life.  
  
And then someone in your crowd asks you, "Do you have any regrets?"  
  
At that point, Neha, you don't want to say:  
  
*I wish I had not tolerated this life for longer than I did.*   
***I wish I had the courage to listen to myself**.*   
*I wish I didn't allow fear to be so much more powerful than my self-belief, or even hope.*   
*I wish I were there with my mom when she passed away.*   
*I wish I weren't far from those I never wanted to be far away from.*   
***I wish I hadn't compared myself to anyone.***   
*I wish I hadn't called myself a failure when I worked really hard every day.*   
***I wish I hadn't judged myself when the world kept doing the same.***  
  
That would be a tragic life, Neha, isn't it?”

## 1 new thing I learnt this week

All 8 billion humans could fit inside a single 1-kilometer cube.   
That’s because the combined volume of all humans is less than half the volume of a cube measuring 1 km on each side.  
  
*For context, the volume of all humans is 4.7×10^8 meters.   
While the volume of a cube that high is about 1×10^9 meters.*  
Of course, this tightly packed cube wouldn’t be livable or humane, but it offers a powerful visual to grasp how small our physical footprint is compared to the size of the planet.
