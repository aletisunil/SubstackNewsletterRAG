**I KNOW THIS WILL SOUND DUMB BUT...**

---

Almost 30-40% of the emails and messages I get, start with either of the following statements:

> I know this will sound dumb, but...

> This will come across as so stupid, but...

> I am so sorry for saying this, but...

And it breaks my heart. It really does break my heart to see people apologizing for who they are. So much so that they are starting a conversation with an apology or a caveat.

​

I heard this somewhere and found it to be so true

> *Do not blame yourself. Because the world will do that any way for you!*

And yet we continue to be apologetic for our words, our thoughts, our feelings, our emotions.

We blame ourselves for feeling what we feel, saying what we say, or even thinking what we think.

​

Why?

Because of our conditioning in life. Because when we did share what we wanted to, in front of our parents, or friends, or relatives, or bosses, or collegues, or fellow students - we were mocked, we were made to feel stupid, we were made to correct ourselves, we were told to shut up, we were told to not think this way.

And over years, that conditioned us.

Conditioned us to constantly over-correct our existence.

​

If it means anything, I want to apologize on behalf of all those who got you to this point.

They perhaps didn't mean it, didn't even realize what their actions would result in.

Forgive them, for they do not know what they have done.

And stop asking for forgiveness.

​

Instead of *"I am sorry this might sound dumb"* , say *"Here is what I think. Tell me what you think"*

Instead of *"I think this is a stupid idea but I am sharing nonetheless"* , say *"Here is an idea. What do you think?"*

Instead of trying to classify your thoughts, let the other person do it for you.

And if they do call it dumb, or stupid - then that is how THEY see it. That is not WHO YOU ARE!

Big difference!

> *I don't know who came here before me, who conditioned you to think you had to apologize.   
> But not here.   
> Dream so big it is silly.   
> Laugh so hard it is obnoxious.   
> Love so much it is impossible.   
> And don't you ever feel unintelligent. And don't you ever apologize. And don't you ever shrink so much you can squeeze yourself into small places and small minds.   
> Grow. It is a big world. There is room. You fit. I promise   
> - Owen Lindley*

**RESULTS OF LAST WEEK'S POLLS**

---

Last week I asked everyone:

For your college days, pick one. And why?   
1. I wish I had studied harder or I want to study hard   
2. I wish I met more people or I want to meet more people

​

80%+ people voted for option No. 2 - which isn't surprising. We all realize, sooner than later in life, that people and the relationships we form drives most of our life. They create the opportunities we sit on, the stories we hear, the thoughts we feel and the emotions we experience.

​

It was interesting, though, to hear the reasons of the 20% who selected "studying harder". For most of them, it is because they felt they missed opportunities in life because of not studying hard. They couldn't get through better companies, or get better jobs, or get better colleges for their masters program. I guess sooner than later they will also realize the truth that the other 80% above have. Matter of time :)

​

And then there were these 1-2% responses which were - BOTH!
