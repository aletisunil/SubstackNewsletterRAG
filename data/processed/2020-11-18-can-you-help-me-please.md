**YOU ARE NOT A LOSER IF YOU ARE IN A JOB**

---

Here is something that bothers me.

The world is increasing indulging in glorification of entrepreneurship.

Everyday, you hear of fund raising announcements. Founders becoming millionaires and billionaires. Sponsoring tournaments, signing up celebrities, giving crazy discounts, running TV ads.

And an entire generation is being raised with the belief that starting up is the cool thing to do.

I don't have a problem with that.

I have a problem when the belief becomes that starting up is the ONLY thing to do.

To the extent that those in jobs are ridiculed for being unambitious, or for not taking control of their life, or being such losers.

No! You are not a loser if you are in a job.

​

The world LOVES to impose prescriptions on us.

If you are doing this, you are successful. If not, you are a loser.

And this is one such prescription.

​

There is nothing wrong in having a job. As long as you are happy in it. And find meaning in it.

What matters is what you want.

You want the stability and structure of a job - go for it!

You want the rush and excitement of a startup - go for it!

You want a life where you do not want to work - go for it!

​

Your race is not with the world. It is not with the one who looks cooler, happier, or is more famous.

Your race is within. It is with your own self. The race is who you were yesterday.

And if you win that race everyday, if you are better than who you were yesterday, you are truly at peace!

​

If you are not winning this race, or worse not even participating in this race with yourself, you will CONSTANTLY fail by falling for what the world thinks you should be doing.

*"Oh my god. What a loser. You are working for someone else. What a sellout. Just because it pays you a salary? What a loser!"*

Don't fall for these words.

*"Why are you not doing these things because these things will make you successful in the eyes of others."*

Don't fall for these words.

**RESULTS OF LAST WEEK'S POLLS**

---

Last week I asked a tricky question and I think most of you fell for the trick.

I asked, whose opinion do you value the most and why?

1. I value my family's opinions the most

2. I value my friend's opinions the most

3. I value the opinions of successful people the most

4. I value my own opinions the most

​

Not surprisingly, almost 80% of you responded with option 4. You value your opinions the most.

However, I do not think that is true (and I am happy to be challenged here).

I got some 500+ email responses and most of them told me how they listen to everyone but they ultimately take the decision that they feel is the best.

Which is VERY DIFFERENT from whose opinion you value the most.

​

Free will is a very interesting concept.

In our life, we think we exercise free will. That our life is a result of the choices we have made. And yes, that is true. But only to the extent of taking those decisions.

The true question is - whose opinions influnced the decision.

If you saw an ad for the latest Redmi phone and decided to buy the phone, is it truly free will? Or were you tricked into that decision by that ad.

If your parents always shared success stories of your elder brother/sister and how proud they were of them, was your decision to follow the footsteps of your elder sibling a decision of free will? Or did your parents influence that?

If your chose to wear that blue tee or dress to the party because you know that your crush loves that color, was it free will?

​

And then it suddenly becomes apparent - oh god - nothing is free will.

We are always influenced by someone or something.

Always!

​

So the question truly was - whose opinions are you almost always influenced by?

And the awareness of this is a very important step towards taking the right decisions.
