**10 things that emotionally intelligent people DO NOT DO!**

1/  
They do not assume that the way they think or react to a situation is the reality.  
They recognise their reactions are merely a reflection of their own biases and understanding.

2/  
They do not blame others for their emotions.  
They understand that they are the ultimate cause of their emotions, not other people.

3/  
They do not fool themselves by believing what will make them happy.  
They recognise that all their predictions of what will make them happy are mere extrapolations of the past.  
So, they are open to new experiences, knowing that everything has parts of good and bad.

4/  
They don’t think that being fearful is a sign they are on the wrong path.  
Instead, they recognise fear as a natural reaction to learning and experiencing something new.

5/   
They are not stuck in the illusion of happiness as a permanent state.   
They recognise that being human means having differing emotions. No feeling is permanent.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Pu7J!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7fd5ecf1-facd-4409-ae74-9e1bce91d8ea_720x720.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Pu7J!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7fd5ecf1-facd-4409-ae74-9e1bce91d8ea_720x720.jpeg)

​

6/   
They do not allow their emotions to be chosen for them.  
They recognise that their reactions are often subject to mob mentality. So, they allow themselves time to process and reflect upon the true origins of their emotions.

7/   
They do not mistake numbness as emotional composure.  
They do not try to control their emotions. They simply try to be aware of them.  
For them, being calm is a skill. Being indifferent isn’t.

8/   
They do not make permanent decisions based on temporary emotions.  
They recognise that decisions taken in the heat of the moment are often misleading.

9/  
They do not cling to relationships just because of time.   
Instead, they value intimacy, value alignment, growth, and vulnerability. And they are courageous enough to exit relationships that stopped being so.

10/ They do not confuse a bad feeling with a bad life.  
They are aware of extrapolation and how this present feeling isn’t reflective of their entire life.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FzqQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4934db41-810c-4076-866b-17f896a2f568_974x974.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FzqQ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4934db41-810c-4076-866b-17f896a2f568_974x974.jpeg)

​

Emotionally intelligent people allow themselves their bad days, their hard moments, and their tough admissions.   
And they give themselves the space to experience them all.   
Knowing that this too shall pass.   
As will the good ones as well.

Being at peace with the transient nature of life is where you find peace.

**Results of last week's survey**

Do you agree with this statement?  
"Your first job in life makes or breaks your career"

1. Absolutely agree
2. Somewhat agree
3. Somewhat disagree
4. Absolutely disagree

Here are the results:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K88n!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2e4164c4-16ab-488e-8745-ab42b99f3956_1049x337.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K88n!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2e4164c4-16ab-488e-8745-ab42b99f3956_1049x337.png)

​

**Fascinating!**

1. Notice how, before folks start working (less than 22 years) - they kind of think that the first job is important. More than 50% agree or somewhat agree.
2. The first phase of work (23-30) then makes it the most equally distributed. Almost half agree and half disagree. This is because the first job DOES impact the first few years.
3. But then - see what happens in 30s and 40s - MASSIVE shift in the realisation that the first job is NOT going to make or break anyone's career.
4. By the time you are in your 40s - only 6% agree with the agree, 19% somewhat agree and 55% absolutely disagree! WOW!

I hope this poll result gives hope to those in their 20s and 30s!

**My response:**​  
The first job doesn't mean anything, except for the first few years.
