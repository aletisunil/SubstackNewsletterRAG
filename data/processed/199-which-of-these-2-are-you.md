# We are all a mix of 2 different people

Everyone is a combination of two distinct personalities:  
  
1. A strategic personality  
2. An operational personality

A strategic personality is somebody who has long-term direction; who has a big-picture outcome in mind.   
  
They know the ‘why’ behind what they're doing.   
They live life like a chess player - planning multiple moves in advance.   
They're great with pattern recognition.   
They're great with systems thinking.   
And that's why you would see them being really good at research, shaping policy, building, let's say, a five-year business plan.

Then, there are people who are really good operationally: with day-to-day operations and day-to-day execution.   
  
They love problem-solving.   
They love processes.   
They are the ones you call when you are in trouble and need instant help.   
They can launch a feature in no time.   
They can run experiments.

Another way of looking at it is: You're at point A.   
You want to go to point B.   
So you need somebody to make a map from point A to point B.   
  
How do you go? What routes do you take? What terrain do you travel on? How do you ensure that it's safe? How do you ensure it's still within the time limit that you want to reach?

Then, there’s somebody who builds the road.   
That person will make all the nuts and bolts happen, get all the equipment, get all the people, and lay down the exact road.

The map-maker cannot do the job of the road-maker, and the road-builder cannot do the job of the map-maker.   
But both of them are critical.

Everyone is not an equal mix of these two.   
But everyone IS a mix of these two.   
The question is: What is your mix?   
  
Are you more of a map-maker?   
Or are you more of a road-builder?

Here are 3 questions that you can ask yourself:

1. When faced with a new challenge, do you first imagine the long-term effects, or do you focus on the immediate next step?   
   If you imagine the long-term effects, you are a strategic thinker or the map-builder.   
   If you identify the immediate next step, you are the operational person.

2. Do you feel more energized designing the plan or carrying it out with your own hands?   
   If you're the designer, then you're, of course, strategic. You're the map-builder.   
   If you love carrying it out, then you're the operational person.

3. When you call something a success, is it because the system works sustainably or because the task was finished efficiently?   
   If you think about the system working sustainably, you are strategic.   
   If you care about the task being finished efficiently, you're operational.

Neither is more right nor more wrong than the other.   
But understanding what you are more of helps you make better choices in life.   
From a point of awareness.

## 1 new thing I learnt this week

Studies show: more exposure to night light; higher risk of cancer.  
The pineal gland produces melatonin in darkness.   
Melatonin helps regulate circadian rhythm and has antioxidant and anti-proliferative properties in lab studies.

Suppressing melatonin (for example, with bright night-time light) is a plausible biological pathway for increased cancer risk.   
*[Source](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6694/16/15/2653)*
