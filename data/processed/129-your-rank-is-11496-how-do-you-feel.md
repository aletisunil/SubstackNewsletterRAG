# It is a matter of how you see it

Which of these options makes you feel better about yourself?  
Option 1: Your rank is #11,496  
Option 2: You are in the top 0.01%

I would assume you will say Option 2 is far, far better. Correct?   
But what if both the options are actually true?

My YouTube Channel on finance has 1.8 billion total views across all my videos.   
Which makes my channel rank #11,496 in the world, when it comes to views.   
  
I could look at that and feel terrible.   
”What a horrible rank,” I’d say.   
EVEN if I take into account that it’s a global ranking.

But there are 115 million YouTube channels in the world, as of Aug 2025.   
115,000,000 channels!   
So a rank of 11,496 puts me in the top 0.01% of all YouTube Channels in the world.   
Not so bad anymore, huh?

That’s the reality of the world we live in today.   
What you see on social media is the rank!   
  
Your colleague’s fancy new car - places them on a higher rank.   
Your friend’s gorgeous partner - places them on a higher rank.   
Your batchmate’s rich international vacation - a higher rank!

We are always sizing people up and coming up with a fictional rank - based on how rich they are, how happy they are, how fit they are, and how good looking they are!

But we rarely stop to ask ourselves - what is our percentage?

Did you know - if you earn 50K per month in India, you are in the top 25% of the country?   
If you earn 1L per month in India, you are in the top 10% of the country?   
If you earn 2L per month, you are in the top 1% of the country?

Did you know that if you have a lovely partner, are financially stable, have a healthy lifestyle, have a good relationship with your parents and siblings, love your job and have time for your friends, family and yourself - you are perhaps in the top 0.00001% of the entire world?

But you instead look at the rank.

**Ranks lie. Context matters.  
Value what truly counts.***[(share on x)](https://ctt.ac/LwJA4)*

Didn’t pick up a new book last week. Was traveling.

The week before, I had read **[The Elements of Choice: Why the Way We Decide Matters](https://amzn.to/3Vwzi48)**, by **Eric J. Johnson**.   
  
It shows how we are all led to make choices that we think are through our own will, but are subtly designed. It is called Choice Architecture.   
  
For example, why are menu items in a restaurant ranked from highest price to lowest? Why are chewing gums and chocolates placed at the store checkout counter?   
Why does PVR offer 3 sizes of popcorn?   
It is all Choice Architecture.   
  
Highly recommended book!
