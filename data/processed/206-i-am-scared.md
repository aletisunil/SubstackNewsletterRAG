# What does 'I am scared' mean?

Whenever you find yourself saying or feeling 'I am scared' it actually means these 3 things, in this order

1. **I will make mistakes**  
   You are new to this.  
   Because of which you will make mistakes.  
   And that scares you, because...
2. **People will laugh at you, and judge you**  
   And that terrifies you.  
   Because you care about what and how people think of you.  
   But, what you don't know as yet is that...
3. **You are doing something that matters**  
   Which is why this thing scares you, in the first place.  
   If it didn't matter, you would be indifferent.  
   But you know you should do it, and you also know that you can't do it as yet, and this combination drives this paralyzing fear.

Fear is natural.  
We often confuse the fear of something happening to us, or our loved ones, with the fear of feeling something (embarrassment, shame).

But they aren't the same.

**The fear/scare of feeling something undesirable - is often the very reason why we should pursue it.  
Because the ONLY way to conquer this fear, is to face it!**[(Share on X)](https://ctt.ac/bYEm1)

## 1 new thing I learnt this week

The brain doesn’t know the difference between reality and imagination.

Visualize biting into a pizza or mango - your mouth may start salivating.  
That’s your brain reacting as if it’s real.  
This is the basis of visualization techniques in sports, therapy, and habit-building.  
Mental rehearsal actually rewires your neurons.
