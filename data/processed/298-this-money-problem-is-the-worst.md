# THIS money mindset really stopped us

When I was studying in the US, I used to get a $1,500 stipend every month.  
It was a rate of ₹50 a dollar, which meant ₹75,000 a month.  
It was a lot of money.

But I didn't wash my clothes in the washing machine.  
I didn't order food. I cooked it myself.  
I didn't travel anywhere in the US for 2 years.  
No phone.   
Not even a lamp for my room.   
I just kept saving.

Because I kept telling myself one thing.  
My parents have worked so hard.  
My parents have suffered so much.  
I can't enjoy my money.  
I don't deserve it.

This is a cage of money.  
A mental cage that stops you from spending.  
A toxic relationship with money that doesn’t help you grow.

And it all starts in childhood.

Every middle-class kid must have heard this in their childhood.  
”Paise ped par nahi ugte.”  
Money doesn't grow on trees.

We didn't know about money, but we knew that it does not grow on trees.  
We wanted toys - *money doesn't grow on trees.*We wanted comics - *money doesn't grow on trees.*We wanted shoes - *money doesn't grow on trees.*

These were not just words.  
This was an emotion.  
Every day, we would see that money does not grow on trees.

Parents used to check price tags.  
They made a budget while buying everything.  
They used to sacrifice a lot of things so that the important things could come home.

And we were convinced that asking for things and wishing for things is wrong.  
We will never have enough money.  
That saving money, preserving money, not spending money - is the right thing.

So we stopped asking for big gifts on birthdays.  
We stopped asking for permission for school trips.  
We stopped asking for clothes.  
We stopped asking for shoes.  
We stopped asking for toys.  
We stopped asking for tuition/coaching money.  
It didn’t feel right.

“Paise ped par nahi ugte.”  
Money doesn't grow on trees.

If you relate to this, then remember one thing.   
Your parents didn’t sacrifice their entire lives, so that you continue to do the same.   
They sacrificed their life to make your life free.   
So make your life free.   
  
Spend.   
Spend when you need to.   
Spend when you want to.   
Spend wisely.   
Spend to make your parents proud.   
And to make yourself proud.

Don’t fall for the scarcity mindset.

Finished reading **[Stories I Must Tell](https://amzn.to/470QoOI)**, by **Kabir Bedi**.   
While I quite liked the book and the way it is written, the stories were unsettling. He had a very different life from what we are used to. Multiple marriages, multiple divorces, distanced from his kids, losing his kid to suicide, multiple affairs, loneliness, stardom, dejection.   
Quite an eventful life - but not a life I would want to live.   
  
Wonderfully written though. Pick it up if you like autobiographies.
