**A new change to the newsletter**

I started this newsletter 156 weeks back (exactly 3 years back) and have never missed a week!   
It started all over the place. I didn't know what to share, how much to share, and how much to ask.

Over time, I found my rhythm.   
The big surprise was people liking the kasams right at the end and the questions I ask every week (and their responses).   
A lot of new changes were also introduced. I eliminated sections and added new ones.

All through the journey though - one thing never changed.  
That I write this newsletter with the aim of helping you.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wC9G!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbea1aa85-6cc7-4111-a91c-e2315a6b35c2_1024x1024.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wC9G!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbea1aa85-6cc7-4111-a91c-e2315a6b35c2_1024x1024.jpeg)

​

And every week - through the hundreds of emails I receive (YES, I do read ALL the replies), I know this aim is being served.

With this 3-year anniversary, I wish to bring one more change.

Make this email about you!

**Every week, you get to ask me questions - these could be personal questions, generic questions, or anything.  
And every week I will pick up 3 questions to answer.   
​**Of course, I will not name you to protect your privacy, and make sure I sanitise parts of the question that could reveal your identity.

While answering, I will delve into my life and experiences, and share how I would approach the same situation.

What will this accomplish?

1. I will pick only those questions that I know will apply to a lot of you
2. I will answer in a way that I know will help a lot of you
3. It will make the email interactive, beyond what it is

How do you ask me your question?

Simple - just reply to this email. No form to be filled, nothing.

Let's see how this works, okay?

Excited about this new change.   
Congrats on the 3rd year anniversary!

# 📕 Book I'm reading this week

Currently reading **[The Greatest Minds and Ideas of All Time](https://amzn.to/3Naz6mw)** by Will Durant.

Will Durant was an American writer, historian, and philosopher. He is best known for The Story of Civilization, 11 volumes written in collaboration with his wife Ariel Durant and published between 1935 and 1975. In this book, he shares with reason, his list of “Hundred Best Books” to the “Ten Greatest Thinkers” to the “Ten Greatest Poets,”. It is quite enjoyable to read this, more so because of the logic Will and Ariel share to get to the final ranking.

When I have moments, I pick up **[Humsafar](https://amzn.to/3CtHAAA)** again - a book on Urdu poetry.   
A few minutes of Urdu poetry and my day is made :))

**Results of last week's survey**

Of all the people you personally know, how smart do you think you are?

1. I will be in the top 1% - I am (almost) the smartest
2. Top 10% - most are not as smart as me
3. Top 50% - half smart, half not so smart
4. Bottom 50% - I know more smart people than me

Here are the results:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!k6wk!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbccf97de-4dd8-4492-99ef-4d4f5fd9b8fa_1040x178.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!k6wk!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbccf97de-4dd8-4492-99ef-4d4f5fd9b8fa_1040x178.png)

​

1. When young, our exposure is limited. So, it's easy to believe that you are smarter than most. 50% of folks below 18 believe they are in the top 10%.
2. You go to college and are like "Holy f - people are smart!" I remember feeling this way when I went to ISB - EVERYONE felt smarter than me.
3. What surprised me was that only 1/4th of folks are surrounded by people smarter than them. I was hoping it to be higher. As is said - you spend time with 3 smart people - you become the fourth.

**My response:**​  
In general, I would say the top 50% (half smart, half not so smart). However, in some aspects that I really care about - I am surrounded by MUCH smarter people. Ruchi (my wife) is exceptional when it comes to processes and organization, my friends are great at spotting red flags that I am terrible at, etc.
