**11 Steps to build a routine in life**

**1/ Start slow** ​  
We often want to make overnight changes.   
But that won’t work.   
Because our body and mind resist the change.

So we have to trick our body and mind.  
By making the change happen slowly.

For example, you wake up at 8 a.m. and want to wake up at 6 a.m. instead?   
Setting the alarm clock for 6 a.m. will work for a few days, until it doesn’t.

Instead, set the alarm for 7:50 a.m. (10 mins earlier than usual).   
Acclimatise your body and mind to this small change.   
It will take 2-3 weeks.   
Post that, another small change.   
Alarm for 7:40 a.m.  
Repeat.   
Then 7:30 a.m.   
Repeat.   
Over a period of a few months, you would get to a routine of 6 a.m.   
Never going back again!

**2/ Measure progress** ​  
Humans do not feel as much joy in hitting a target, as they do in working towards a target.

If you find a way to measure progress along the way, you will enjoy the routine a lot more than reaching the destination, when you do.

For example: Reading can be a daunting and difficult routine to establish. because we attach the joy to finishing a book.

Try this instead.   
Read 1 page in a day.   
Same the next day.   
Same the next day.   
Don’t have to read more.   
Just read a page a day.   
And document that progress.

Put a big calendar on the wall and cross out the day you read a page.  
When you see that big calendar with multiple crosses in a streak - THAT will make you feel like a winner!   
Irrespective of how much of the book you have finished.

**3/ The 2-min rule   
​**The hardest thing about setting a routine is getting started.

The 2-minute rule says - whatever it is that you want to set as a routine, do it for just 2 mins.

Slowly, you will get used to it.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3hp0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fabd26cc6-e60d-464f-9356-aa21bba06c97_1024x1024.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3hp0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fabd26cc6-e60d-464f-9356-aa21bba06c97_1024x1024.jpeg)

​

**4/ Temptation bundling   
​**Bundle what you like to do and what you want to do as part of a routine.

For example: You like watching YouTube, but you want to study as a routine.   
You will then say - I will watch my course content on YouTube - whatever it is - tutorials, discussions, animations.

**5/ Habit stacking   
​**Combine what you are already habituated to, with something you want to build a habit for.

For example: Let’s say you want to set a skincare routine. So you could say, as soon as I have brushed my teeth (which is, I hope, a habit already), I will start my skin routine.

**6/ Win the week   
​**We often want to win EVERY day.   
Do everything that we want to do, in a single day itself.   
We can’t. Don’t try to.

Instead, plan your week and try to do everything you want to do, within a week.   
You don’t have to exercise every day. Exercise only on weekends.   
You do not have to read every day. Read only on weekends.   
You do not have to wait for the weekend to watch movies. Watch 15 mins every day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!AwBD!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fff11ce83-07e1-45b5-a9be-97df4f576420_1024x1024.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!AwBD!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fff11ce83-07e1-45b5-a9be-97df4f576420_1024x1024.jpeg)

​

**7/ Reward yourself   
​**If you do what you want to do, give yourself a pat on the back. Reward yourself with something you would cherish.

For example: I have distraction time scheduled for 3 slots every day, for 30 mins each. During this time, I can waste time. Do whatever I want.   
This is my reward for doing all the things I have to do in the day.

**8/ Build in public   
​**Publicly announce your habit. Make yourself accountable to someone outside of you.

For example: You tell your friend - I will send you a pic of my watch at the time I get up every day. Else, I will pay you XX amount.

**9/ Pre-commitment   
​**You are more likely to follow through, if you already have some form of commitment towards it.

For example: Paying in advance for the gym membership, the book club, the sports coaching or the course - will make it more likely for you to follow through and finish it.

**10/ Stay flexible   
​**A routine is not designed to make you emotionless or rigid.   
It is ultimately your tool to accomplish what you wish. You are the master.

Stay flexible. It’s okay if you broke the routine today. Come back to it tomorrow. It’s fine. You will be okay.

**11/ What if?   
​**Close your eyes and visualise what will happen if you are unable to build this routine.   
More importantly, if you do build this routine - how will it change your life?

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xxYf!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3fa2f09d-b27c-4a6a-a844-a68273193962_1024x1024.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xxYf!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3fa2f09d-b27c-4a6a-a844-a68273193962_1024x1024.jpeg)

​

A routine is often mistaken as getting up early, having a set of time-bound tasks, clinically going through them, and moving on from one to another.

No!

A routine is your mechanism to accomplish what you feel is important for you in your life. It is a method to objectively work towards it - slowly and steadily, every day, week, month, year.

Building one can be the best gift you can give yourself.

**“The secret of your future is hidden in your routine”

# 📕 Book I'm reading this week

Finished reading **[The Inner Game of Tennis](https://amzn.eu/d/6WBEs1B) by Timothy Gallwey**.

Quite a simple and stunning read about the mindset while playing any sport (not just tennis).   
For example, often you find players talking to themselves while playing - say "C'mon Ankur", "Focus Ankur", "This is not the way to play."  
In that moment, who are they speaking to?   
Are there suddenly two people inside our heads - the speaker and the listener? Or the observer of the game and the doer of the game?  
Timothy argues yes. The book discusses how Player 1 and Player 2 can work with each other to master the game.   
Loved it!

Also, I had an IG live with [Shane Parrish](https://fs.blog/about/) who has recently released his first book - **[Clear Thinking](https://amzn.to/48ZSkWk).** ​  
To prep for the conversation, I requested for an early copy of the book and read it.   
Powerful read on what sets us up to make better decisions in life, right from what are the usual enemies of clear thinking to the process of making wise decisions.   
Good book!
