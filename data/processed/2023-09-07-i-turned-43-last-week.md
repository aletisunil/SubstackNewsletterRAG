Currently reading **[Several short sentences about writing](https://amzn.to/44EmEm4)**, by Verlyn Klinkenborg. The book is quite unreal - BUT also not a book for everyone. If you are not an active reader, then this book will be a waste for you to become a better writer (see pics below).

For those who are active readers and would love to write - pick this up. Brilliant read. Just my style of writing.

**Results of last week's survey**

Would you like to, or have you ever gone on a solo trip?   
Completely alone - no one else.

1. I have
2. I haven't + I want to
3. I haven't + I don't want to
4. I am not sure

Here are the results:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_LD1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F419ec01d-0928-46af-98a3-e7ebb5033fb6_868x180.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_LD1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F419ec01d-0928-46af-98a3-e7ebb5033fb6_868x180.png)

​

1. With age, the share of people who have gone on a solo trip goes up - I am surprised, though, at the share—40% beyond 30s.
2. Also interesting is the increase in the share of people who do not want to go on a solo trip, as they age. You just realise that it's not your thing.

**My response:**​  
I have been on several solo work trips (for extended periods as well) but never on a solo leisure trip. I am not sure if I want to :))
