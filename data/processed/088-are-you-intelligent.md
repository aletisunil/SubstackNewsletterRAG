# Intelligence isn’t what we think it is

A lot of us think of intelligence as IQ.   
Our ability to understand things, to grasp them well, and to learn them quickly.   
And then, we attach outcomes to this capability.

In school, intelligent kids understood the subject well and then, as an outcome, scored well in exams.   
In college, intelligent kids prepared well and then, as an outcome, got the best-paying jobs.   
At work, intelligent colleagues did their work immaculately and then, as an outcome, got promoted, got increments and bonuses.   
In life, intelligent people figure things out, network easily, understand how the world works, and then as an outcome, they are successful: money-wise, fame-wise, respect-wise.

For the longest time, I believed the same.   
For me, the smartest kids in school and college were the toppers, and I was convinced they would go on to make the most successful people in the future.

Then, I got to experience life myself.   
And I saw a disconnect.

The intelligent folks were making money and were branded successful - sure.   
But it wasn’t that all of them were happy.   
In fact, most didn’t seem so.   
Instead, they were busy. Super busy.   
Hustling.   
Winning.   
Making things work.   
Making sure the world continues to see them as intelligent.

And there was this group.   
They didn’t necessarily top the exams.   
They didn’t always get the best-paying jobs.   
They didn’t have to have the fanciest cars or buy the latest phones.   
But… I saw them doing something different.

They were intensely hard on themselves.   
Not because they hated themselves.   
Instead, because they LOVED themselves.

They loved themselves so much that they always wanted the best for themselves.

So they did whatever was required to make that happen.   
To become the person they always wanted to become.   
To be as happy as they always thought they deserved to be.

And that, in my definition, is true intelligence.   
The ability to live life the way YOU WANTED TO ALWAYS LIVE.

> Because if you don’t have a life that you want, can you even call yourself intelligent?

## 1 new thing I learnt this week

Mosquito repellents don't repel mosquitoes. Instead, they hide you.   
The spray blocks the mosquito's sensors so they don't know you are there.  
Mosquitoes find you mainly by sensing carbon dioxide, body odour compounds (like lactic acid and ammonia), and heat.  
Repellents confuse or block the mosquito’s olfactory receptors. Without those signals, the mosquito has a harder time realizing there’s a warm-blooded human nearby, so it’s less likely to land on you.
