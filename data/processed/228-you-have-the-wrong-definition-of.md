# THIS Opinion Is The Difference Between Success and Failure

At some point in life, we define what success means to us.   
For most, it is money.   
For some, professional status.   
For others, purpose.   
Whatever it is, we see it as a finish line.

The mistake most people make is to think of this finish line as a single point.  
One exact spot on a graph.   
And that the only way to get there is through one straight line from where they are today.

That “straight line” is usually the path we’ve seen others take, or the one we’ve been told is the right path.   
In school, it often looks like this:   
Good grades → Good college → Good job → Rising income → Wealth = Finish Line.

But here’s what successful people realize:   
The finish line isn’t a point.   
It’s a line.   
A vertical line on a graph.

Touch ANY point on that line, and you’ve reached your definition of success.   
Which means from where you are today, **there are not one, but infinite paths to become successful**.

The straight path may be the shortest, but it is also the most crowded.   
Everyone wants to be on it.   
The truly successful know that sometimes, the better path is the longer one, the unconventional one, the one that fits their life better.   
They are not afraid to change paths when needed.

> **Those who believe there is only one way to succeed almost always get stuck. Those who succeed are the ones who dare to find another way.

## 1 new thing I learnt this week

There is a difference between flag hoisting and unfurling the flag.   
**Hoisting**: Raising the flag up the flagpole from a lower position to the top.   
Which is why we hoist the flag on 15th August (by the PM), because that is when the country got its independence.   
**Unfurling**: Opening up or spreading the flag so it becomes visible.  
Which is what the President does on 26th January. By this date in 1950, India was already an independent nation; the flag was already “up” in spirit.
