**THE ONE WORD THAT DEFINES SUCCESS**

---

It is not hard work. It is not luck. It is not intelligence. It is not money. It is none of these.

It is the word **"No"**

Let me share a story, that you most likely have already heard. It is one of my favorite ones.

In Hindu mythology, in the epic battle story of Mahabharat, there lived 5 brothers called the Pandavas. One of them, Arjun, was considered the best archer in the world.

One day, while being trained for archery, their guru, named Guru Dhronacharya, placed a toy bird on top of a tree branch and asked the brothers to come one by one and shoot the eye of the bird with their arrow.

First came the eldest brother, Yudhisthira. He took his aim and the Guru then asked him.

*"What all do you see, Yudhisthira?"*

*"I see the bird guruji, the leaves, the clouds."*

*"Fair enough. You may give way to your brother."*

And thus, one by one, the first four brothers came, took their aim, were asked what do they see and they replied - "the bird, the leaves, the tree, the sky, the clouds."

​

And then came Arjun.

He took his aim.

*"What all do you see Arjun?"*

*"I see the bird's eye, Guruji"*

*"No. I asked what all do you see. Tell me everything that you see."*

*"I only see the bird's eye, Guruji"*

The guru smiled and asked Arjun to shoot.

​

*(Confession? I broke down while typing this story. Not sure why. Something about people being intently focused on what they have to do, makes me cry.)*

*​*

Everyday I get 200+ emails. I read ALL of them ( *mummy swear!* )

I reply to 30-40 of them. And in 90% of those emails, my answer is no.

*"I am completely blocked. Sorry, won't be able to do this."*

*"This is not priority for me right now. Sorry."*

*"This will be a no for me. Not something I can add value to."*

*​*

The more I say NO to things that do not matter, the more I can yes to the things that DO matter.

That is what Arjun did.

He said NO to everything else that was meaningless.

And thus said YES to the bird's eye.

​

His focus was his success.

And focus doesn't come from intelligence, or time, or money, or habit.

**Focus is a choice.**

A choice you make that you want to say no to everything else that isn't important.

​

An interesting question that emerges is - what makes us say yes in the first place?

And I know a lot of you will say - I don't have a choice.

*My boss asked me to do something just when I was about to go to bed.*

*My friend called me right when I sat down to study.*

*Some house work came up just when I sat down to read.*

All of them seem legit reasons.

But here is the truth - the dark truth.

When you say yes - you are doing it out of will.

You are doing it to upload a certain image of yours that you do not wish to disturb.

You are saying yes, because you want the person opposite to you to think of you in a certain way.

And that other person could also be you yourself :)

​

You say yes to a new thing, because you want to see yourself as someone who is excited about new opportunities.

You say yes to a new Netflix series, because you want to see yourself not experience FOMO.

You say yes to sleeping more, because you want to live with that "I am so tired and I need rest" story of yours.

​

Say more NOs than YESs in life.

Focus on the one thing that is most important.

**RESPONSES TO LAST WEEK'S POLLS**

---

So last week I asked, what turned out to be a controversial question.

Which of these is true and why do you think it is true?

1. The most studious kids in my school are doing the best in life

2. The naughtiest kids in my school are doing the best in life

​

Almost 70% of the responses hinted that it were the naughtiest kids that are doing best in life. The rest was for studious.

The reasons were interesting.

People felt that it was the naughty kids that took more risks, were better exposed to realities of the world, experienced a lot more and thus in some way set themselves up for success later in their lives.

While that in favor of the studious kids said that they developed the habit of being disciplined, committed and diligent early on in life and that set them up for success.

Clearly both sides are right.

​

There were a LOT of you who wrote in questioning what is "doing the best in life".

People asked me for my definition (and I asked them back for theirs!), people felt that the distinction didn't even make sense (how could being naughty or studious define success) and some were even offended (I am assuming it was the studious ones!).

Overall, I liked the engagement this poll created. Thanks for indulging.
