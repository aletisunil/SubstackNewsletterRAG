**WHAT IT MEANS TO APOLOGIZE**

---

The other day I got an email from someone who was guilty of a failed relationship. He realized he hadn't been the best partner and because of that they broke up.   
And he had tried to apologize to her, for his action, but she had clearly moved on.   
​   
He wrote to me asking what else could he do, to bring her back in his life?   
​   
I told him - nothing!   
He could do nothing to bring her back.   
BUT   
He should do one thing nonetheless.   
Apologize again.   
NOT with the intent of bringing her back.   
NOT with the intent of coming clean.   
NOT with the intent of trying to fix things.   
Instead, only with the intent to say sorry.   
That's it!   
​   
One of the toughest things that humans need to do is apologize.   
But it is also one of the most powerful things we can do.   
Most of us get it wrong though.   
​   
Because   
- we apologize so that the other person accepts us back   
- we apologize out of fear of authority   
- we apologize because of fear of judgement   
All the wrong reasons   
​   
A sincere apology comprises 3 parts   
1. I am sorry for my actions   
2. I take responsibility   
3. Is there anything I can do to make it right?   
​   
Saying all these three things is under your control.   
However, the response isn't.   
Especially to no.3 above.   
​   
It is your duty to ask if there is anything you can do to make it right.   
But it is not your right to get an answer.   
That is the other's person's choice.   
​   
If you get it, you may see your apology turn into not just forgiveness but also acceptance.   
If you do not get any response, your apology should still come!   
​   
The next time you apologize, ask yourself why are you doing it?   
And if there is any selfish reason behind it - then it is a fake apology.   
​   
​ **True apology does not come with any expectations.** ​

​ **RESPONSES TO THE POLL LAST WEEK**

---

Last week I asked all of you a very interesting question   
Which one will you choose and why?   
1. Go back and change the past   
2. See the future (but you can't change it)   
​   
50%+ chose option 1!   
And almost all of it was to rectify mistakes.   
People wanted to change the college they went to, the friends they made, the relationships they got into, the companies they joined.   
Thank you for sharing all your lovely stories.   
​   
Interestingly, 30% chose neither   
They were happy with the past (or at least realized they were a result of their past so didn't want to change it) and didn't want to remove the excitement of the future by witnessing it.   
I have never had a poll where people completely ignored my choices and choose a new option.   
LOVE IT!   
​   
Around 20% chose witnessing the future.   
It seemed most of them did so grudgingly ( *aur koi choice nahi hai!* ) - haha   
​   
So many of you wrote in to say, the poll made them think.
