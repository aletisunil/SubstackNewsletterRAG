# New Year is a weird concept

We have all had that moment where we sat down to study during school, realized the time is 6:47pm and said to ourselves, I will start at 7pm!  
The same thing happens when at 11:53pm in the night we tells ourselves “ok, just 7 more minutes of reels, before I sleep”.  
It is oddly satisfying to round off time.  
Especially when it comes to something we know we have to do or something we know we have to stop doing.

It is almost as if time has an emotion.  
As if 11:47 is less capable, less ready than 12:00 :))

But time is time.

On 31st December, as midnight strikes, there is no change in nature.   
The sun and moon are still doing their thing.   
The plants are doing their thing.   
So are the animals.   
Yet, almost all of humankind gathers around to celebrate the rounding off of a year.

As if 1st Jan will bring with it a new time.   
But it is all in our head.

Which is what makes it fascinating.   
Because ‘our head’ is a powerful thing.   
It can make us do things we didn’t know we were capable of.   
And it can stop us from doing things that were well within our reach.

So many of us use the New Year as an opportunity.  
To reset a bad time gone by.   
To focus on an important time ahead.  
To set goals/targets.   
To make a plan on how to hit them.   
To make promises to ourselves with a renewed sense of rigor.

And you know what - that’s awesome.   
It is stupid, but it is awesome.

Stupid, because time doesn’t care.   
31st Dec and 1st Jan are no different from any other date or moment in time.   
Awesome, because you cutely choose to ignore this, and give the new year importance that it doesn’t deserve.   
And build your life around this moment.

So this New Year, I wish you clarity.  
Not just motivation.

I wish you courage.  
Not just goals.

And I hope you remember this:  
**YOU have the power to give any day just as much importance as you give the start of a new year.   
If a date can make you begin, a random Tuesday can keep you going.

May you fall in love with who you are becoming.

May this year called 2026 be special.

## 1 new thing I learnt this week

The word salary comes from the Latin word salarium.  
Salarium was the allowance Roman soldiers received to buy salt.

Salt was not seasoning back then. It was survival.  
• It preserved food  
• It prevented disease  
• It enabled long military campaigns  
• It was scarce and controlled

Salt was so valuable that parts of the Roman economy literally ran on it.
