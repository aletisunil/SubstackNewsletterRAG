**WHY DO WE LOVE FESTIVALS**

---

Because everyone is nice to each other.  
Because everyone is dressed nicely.  
Because everyone gets a few holidays.  
Because everyone gets something sweet to eat.  
Because everyone is happy.

Festivals, are rarely about the occasion now.   
They are about the experience.

Reminding us of a time when we were like this, mostly.  
When we were kids.  
When we were nice to each other.  
When we didn't care what we had worn.  
When everyday was a holiday.  
When we enjoyed what we ate.  
When everyone was happy.

And then we grew up!

And now we need festivals, as an excuse to be able to feel that feeling again.

I wish you all a happy festive season.  
Not today.  
Everyday!

Make it happen :)

**BOOK I AM READING THIS WEEK**

---

Read 2 amazing books this week.

The first one, yet to be released, is by Devdutt Pattanaik  
​[Hope: Wisdom to Survive in a Hopeless World](https://amzn.to/3q6Z3tp)​

Devdutt writes about mythology, and in this book about hope, he reminds us how the world has "always" been starving for hope. And how have people, including the "gods" that we know of, found hope in the unlikeliest of places.

A simple yet powerful read, if you would love to know more about the journey of hope. And how in today's tough world, you can still find it.

The second I read is from my ISB Batchmate Srihari.  
​[Strike your balance: How to be at peace with yourself](https://amzn.to/3k2VwbQ)​

Srihari has worked with some of the world's best known brands - Dell, Microsoft, Adobe, in the field of product marketing. But in this book he speaks about the balance of a professional and an individual with their own desires, needs and interests. We could potentially find ourselves at conflict with our professional identifies and this immensely powerful book lays down simple steps to strike the balance.

Great read for working professionals.

**RESPONSES TO LAST WEEK'S QUESTION**

---

Last week I asked all of you:

How much time do you spend daily, on your phone?

1. Less than 1 hour
2. 1-3 hours
3. 3-5 hours
4. 5+ hours

Here are the results:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LhPX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbd967adb-fe0c-436b-96fa-9f765e3c6e6c_766x148.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LhPX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbd967adb-fe0c-436b-96fa-9f765e3c6e6c_766x148.png)

​

Observations:

1. As we grow old, we spend lesser time on the phone. Understandable.

2. The sweet spot, up to 22 years - is around 5 hours, which drops to around 3-5 hours for 23-40, and to around 3 hours for 40+.

3. Less than 5% of the overall population (massive extrapolation, from just this survey) is spending less than 1 hour on their phones. Pause and think of this for a second. A product that is used by 95%, for more than an hour a day. INCREDIBLE!
