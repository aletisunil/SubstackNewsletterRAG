# Middle-class life is terrible

I received an email today:  
  
*I’m 29, still in training, and I’ve managed to build around ₹8L in mutual funds so far. Recently, while discussing finances with my parents, I realized something that’s been bothering me.*  
  
*As a family, we have savings of roughly ₹70L. No big real estate - just one house and a small shop that runs. Yet our lifestyle is, frankly, lower middle class. We avoid spending on anything beyond absolute necessity. Even small comforts feel like indulgences.*  
  
*And that made me pause.*  
  
***If people with decades of discipline and a respectable corpus still live in constant restraint, then what exactly are we building towards?***  
*I understand we are nowhere near “financially free.” But is there a point where one should say:*  
*“Okay, I am on track. I can invest and allow myself a better life”?*  
  
*How does one define that balance?*  
  
*What should be the goal - a number, a milestone, a feeling?*  
  
*Because sometimes, it feels like we’re running a marathon where the finish line keeps moving, and life is passing by quietly on the side.*  
  
This was so relatable.   
Years of middle-class conditioning makes spending money feel guilty.   
Especially if it is spending on our own selves.   
For things that are not "long-term" or "useful".  
  
**Here was my exact response:**  
  
This is a very valid concern, Karan (Name changed).   
The only way to deal with this is to slowly practice indulgence.  
  
Assign a guilt-free quantum every month - say X% of the household income - towards this guilt-free spend.   
  
Do not define towards what - just define the amount.  
  
You will find that early on, it will be hard to even spend this money.   
Because of the mindset and conditioning you described.  
But slowly, the spends will happen.  
Then slowly, the nature of spends will change.   
  
From consumables, it will move to goods (say mobile phones or TV) to eventually experiences (such as travel).  
  
It will take time, but the seeds will have to be sown by deciding the guilt-free amount first.  
  
I hope this helps.
