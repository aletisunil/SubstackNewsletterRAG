# Winning People Without Losing Yourself

Since our early years, we have been taught that it is wrong to be selfish, it is wrong to place yourself in front of others, and it is wrong to desire before fulfilling the needs of others.   
Especially women.

So we do that.   
We curb our own desires.   
We look down upon people whom we think are selfish.   
We place others in front of us.

*And slowly, this habit becomes a lifestyle.*

We feel guilty buying something for ourselves if we haven’t bought anything for our family.   
We find it impossible to say no to others so that we don’t displease them.   
We please others so that they like us.   
We do not confront; instead, keep absorbing the harm.  
We tolerate toxicity, assuming there must be something wrong in us.

*I was like this - for the longest time.* 

A people pleaser.   
One with the saviour mindset.   
One always helping others, even when I couldn’t.   
Always saying yes, even when I didn’t want to.

And I realized it was exhausting.

***So I asked myself - is it not possible to be a nice human to others, while attending to my own needs as well?*** 

That led me to a different path.   
A different mindset.   
A different approach towards people.

*My latest book is about this journey.*
