**Question from Chinmay  
How do we stop comparing ourselves to other people?**

We fall prey to such comparisons because we have built our worldview on two false assumptions instead of understanding the basic facts of life.  
​  
​False Assumption #1: The world is a zero-sum game.​  
What does a zero-sum game mean?  
It means that for one to win, another has to lose.  
If my score is +1, your score has to be -1, so that the sum is zero.  
This is NOT true.

The world has abundant opportunities.  
All of us can create as many wins for ourselves instead of waiting for someone else to lose.

​

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SxSR!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5c24fa84-b206-4705-9a6c-3d943416d7cc_1024x1024.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SxSR!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5c24fa84-b206-4705-9a6c-3d943416d7cc_1024x1024.jpeg)

​

False Assumption #2: All of us have the same opportunities.​  
This is NOT true.

There is no one, absolutely no one on planet Earth who has the same characteristics as you do.  
Because of which, all of us come across very different opportunities.  
This means, by default, we will all end up at different places because we started off differently.  
Your temperament, your capability, your choices, where you were born, your family, your upbringing, your luck – all of them are so unique to you that by design, no one can be like you.   
Since everyone started differently, everyone, by default, will end up differently.

So, whenever I see anyone else winning and I compare myself to them, I ask myself:  
If they earn more than me, does that mean it will be true forever?  
If they have a job and I do not, does that mean I will never have a job?  
If they happen to be in a lovely relationship, does that mean I will be lonely forever?  
The answer to all of these questions is no.

And that drives me to work harder, to fix the things that I should fix, and realize that they have something I wanted; and if I tried, I could get it too.

​

**Question from Anubha  
How should I decide if a job opportunity is right for me or not?**

I would ask 3 questions, in order of priority:

1. Is this a place where I will learn something that I want to learn, but do not know enough of right now?   
I will find that out by asking endless questions about my role, the nature of work, and why they think I can be a good fit for it.   
2. If there is a culture that will support my personality?  
I will find that out by speaking to alums of the organization (through LinkedIn), reading Glassdoor reviews, and if possible, speaking to current employees as well.  
3. What does this company offer that my current company doesn't? How valuable is that?  
I will find that out by listing the differences, ranking them in order of priority, and asking myself if the top 3 differences weigh more than the top 3 things that my current company offers which the new company doesn't.

​
