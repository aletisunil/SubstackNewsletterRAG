# AI is changing jobs

Jeff Bezos, founder of Amazon, wrote in one of the annual shareholder letters:  
  
*”People often ask, what is going to change?”  
”We at Amazon asked a different question instead - **What is never going to change?**”  
”And we identified 3 things.”  
  
”One - **people are never going to say - I wish things were more expensive at Amazon**. So people will always want value.”  
  
”Second - **people are never going to say - I wish there were lesser items listed** on Amazon. So people will always want assortment.”  
  
”And third - **people are never going to say - I wish my product came late**. So people will always want fast delivery.”  
  
”Which means, if we focus on the biggest assortment providing the maximum value delivered in the fastest time - we will always be working towards the right thing.”  
”And that’s what we do at Amazon.”*

Similarly, the wrong question to ask is “Will AI take away my job, or is AI changing jobs?”  
The answer is conclusively yes.   
But that answer only scares us.   
It doesn’t tell us what to do.

But if we asked a different question instead:  
**”What are things that will always be needed, with or without AI?”**  
We might begin to find the answers we want.

For example, the need for communication will never change or end.   
The mode of communication will keep changing.   
There were letters at some point.   
Then there were typewriters.   
Then pagers.   
Then SMS.   
Then WhatsApp.   
Today social media.   
Tomorrow - who knows!

So if you focused on becoming the best at communication - you will have to keep evolving.   
By learning writing first, then typewriting, then computer typing, then mobile typing, then social media, tomorrow agentic AI, and something else the day after.

As the world changes, the mode changes too.   
But the need to communicate will not.

I often say this:  
**Jobs will not be eliminated by AI.   
Jobs will be taken over by someone using AI.  
[(Share on X)](https://ctt.ac/w6tsf)**

The world will always need communicators, decision makers, story-tellers, artists, engineers, doctors, lawyers, and accountants.   
But how they serve will change.   
How they work will change.

And if you are one, you will have to change too.

## 1 new thing I learnt this week

The smell of rain isn’t rain.   
It is a molecule called Geosmin, produced by soil bacteria.   
When raindrops hit dry soil, they launch microscopic aerosols carrying geosmin into air.   
Scientists think this sensitivity helped early humans find water.
