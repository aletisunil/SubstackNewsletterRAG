**Are you mentally strong?**

Tennis is a weird game, when it comes to keeping score.   
Both players start a game at 0-0 (called love-all, in Tennis language, love meaning zero)  
When one player wins a point the score doesn't read 1-0. No! It reads 15-0 (15 love)  
Another point and it becomes 30-0.  
Another point and one would expect 45-0 but no no no - it's 40-0!  
Another point and the player wins the game.

Weird, as I mentioned.

If the opponent wins a point after the first one was won by you, the score becomes 15-15.   
And so it can also become 30-30.

30-30 is an interesting point in tennis. It's called a pressure point.   
That's because whosoever wins the next point, will go to 40-30 and has an advantage to win the game (with just one more point).   
The point is called a pressure point because at this stage the true mettle of the player is tested.

​

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NgKW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fca44a083-017f-4382-9a49-c534c90998ee_1024x1024.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NgKW!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fca44a083-017f-4382-9a49-c534c90998ee_1024x1024.jpeg)

​

I have personally felt it.   
I play tennis 6 days a week and have been playing for years now - but at 30-30 I still have that strange feeling in my stomach.   
That feeling that the game could go in any direction from here.   
What if I lose the next point?   
It will be a psychological victory for my opponent. That may translate into an actual victory for them.

Studies have shown that at this pressure point, even the best players succumb.   
The only ones who are able to hold their nerve are the ones with mental strength.   
Who not only know that this is just another point but also CONVINCE themselves that this is just another point.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kOvP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7352778a-ca3a-4648-9463-71b80529f2fa_1024x1024.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kOvP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7352778a-ca3a-4648-9463-71b80529f2fa_1024x1024.jpeg)

​

That is the short definition of mental strength.   
The ability to tell yourself a story that you know is true.   
Because think about it - how many times do we know what's right for us and YET we are unable to do it?   
Because our mind convinces us as against the other way around.

And in this example of Tennis' pressure point lies the story of all of us.   
That interview we were scared to fail?  
That exam we were scared to fail?  
That presentation we were scared to fail?  
That relationship we were scared to fail?

All of them are pressure points.   
Moments that we make into do-or-die moments.  
Moments where we convince ourselves that if we fail, there is a point of no return.  
So we get scared.  
We succumb to pressure.  
We offer psychological victory to our opponent. Which could very well be our own mind.

And in such moments, your skill, capability, and preparation do not matter as much as your mental strength does.  
The story you are telling yourself.   
The story that you tell your mind, instead of the other way round.

Pressure points are life's way to test how well we have prepared ourselves.   
Not for the moment. For life!
