**Question from Ashish  
How to be consistent with your learning goals?**

Imagine it's the 1st of January.  
And I want to gift you all something - a gym membership! Yay!!   
But this is a unique gym.  
Unique because of 3 things:

1. It does not have any mirrors. So you cannot see yourself.
2. It doesn't have any weighing scale or measuring tape. So you cannot measure yourself.
3. And the most unique thing - when you go to the gym, you are all alone. There is NO ONE ELSE in the gym. Just you.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dNEt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2136defe-0338-4ad9-827f-e8eebbe24309_1024x1024.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dNEt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2136defe-0338-4ad9-827f-e8eebbe24309_1024x1024.jpeg)

​

My question to you is - how many days will you go to this gym?  
My prediction for your answer will be - I won't go for more than a week.   
What if I told you that during this week, you were actually doing quite well? You were getting fit and losing weight.   
Would you still stop going after a week or so?  
The answer would be yes.

You know why?  
Because this gym doesn't give you the most basic need of every human - a sense of progress.   
We feel good NOT when we achieve things.   
We feel good when we work towards the things we want to achieve.   
We feel good when we know we are progressing.

So, the ONLY thing that will help you be consistent in your learning goals is to build a sense of progress in it.​  
Progress that you feel good about, progress that you know will help you achieve what you want.

**Question from Sahil  
I do not have any friends. I feel lonely. How do I get over this?**

We feel lonely without others because we have not built a friendship with our own selves.   
Who else can be your best friend, but you?   
You know your strengths. You know your weaknesses. You know your secrets. You know your desires. You know your mind.

The truth is - the less we know ourselves, the more we seek to know ourselves through others.   
But they do not know us.   
They know a small part of us.   
But we will form an entire image of ourselves, through the limited view that they have of our life.

Does this mean we do not need friends?   
Of course not. We need friends.   
If one is friends with their own self, then one will still benefit from friends.   
But we seem to need them because we think we are alone and need to share our thoughts, sorrows, and experiences.   
That is an excuse to not be able to do so with your own self.

Become your best friend.

**Question from Smriti  
You seem to rent your car. What is the point of money, if you cannot drive a nice car?**

1. Just because I rent a car doesn't mean it's not a nice car. It is a nice car, according to our needs.
2. I think of money as 3 things:
   
   1. Freedom - to do what I want to do.   
      For example, I work from home, we take frequent vacations, I do not work for anyone except myself, I take afternoon naps, and I have a lot of free time on my hands. It is a life I have built for myself.
   2. Abstraction - to not have to spend time on things I do not want to do.  
      So we have a cook, house help, and a driver. I would happily pay for convenience and predictability. Because these are things that I value.
   3. Life satisfaction - to do things that bring you joy.  
      We spend A LOT of money on travel every year. That is our "nice car".

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0eCz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F033e3fcb-f453-43b0-82d3-a49679e16d46_1024x1024.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0eCz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F033e3fcb-f453-43b0-82d3-a49679e16d46_1024x1024.jpeg)

​

For me, I do not value a car. It is just a vehicle to take me from point A to B. As long as it does it safely and comfortably, I couldn't care less which car or brand it is. Least of all, buy an expensive one to show I have money.

I value experiences a lot.   
Travelling, live events, good food, team offsites, health, fitness, gifting people, and helping people - these are things we love spending money on.   
That makes me rich, in my definition.

1. The younger you are, the likelier it is that someone cooks your food.

**My response:**​  
We have a cook at home. It is, in my opinion, the best use of our money - to get fresh food, generate employment, and save on our time.
