**Life is about creating options**

Go back to your college days.  
Imagine the time of placements.   
And imagine two scenarios.

Scenario I:   
You have given multiple interviews but haven't cleared any yet.   
You have one last interview scheduled tomorrow.   
And you feel some pressure. Let's call it Pressure 1 or P1.

Scenario 2:  
You have given multiple interviews but haven't cleared any yet.   
However, anticipating that, you reached out to a friend for a referral in the company she works for.   
You went through the process off-campus.   
And got an offer from them.   
It's not really a place you fancy yourself working at, but what the heck?   
You also have one last interview scheduled tomorrow, as part of the college placements.   
And you feel some pressure. Let's call it Pressure 2or P2.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fiSO!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa5df9276-a9f2-4d49-84e4-a3cc377c4b9f_1024x1024.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fiSO!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa5df9276-a9f2-4d49-84e4-a3cc377c4b9f_1024x1024.jpeg)

​

My question to you.   
Which is higher?   
P1 or P2?

I would argue P1 > P2.

Not by a lot, but it is.

Now imagine you go into the final interview and perform to the best of your ability.   
Let's call these performances P3 and P4 (corresponding to Scenario 1 and 2 respectively).

My question to you.   
Which is higher?   
P3 or P4?

I would argue P3 < P4.   
Your performance in the first scenario is lower than your performance in the second.   
Because of the pressure you feel.   
In fact, I will argue it is not just lower.   
It is MUCH lower.   
P3 << P4.

Now imagine you clear both the interviews. Yay!  
You join an organization.   
But sadly, you are not happy, in both scenarios.   
The work is not exciting. The pay is not exciting (assuming equal pay for both jobs). The growth isn't there.   
And you feel pain.   
Let's call it P5 and P6 (for first and second jobs respectively).

My question to you.   
Which is higher?   
P5 or P6?

I would argue P5 > P6.   
The pain you feel in the first job will be higher than the pain you feel in the second.   
Because you feel more stuck in the first scenario.   
You feel you did not have a choice.   
You HAD TO take this job.   
Without your wish.

In fact, I will argue it is not just higher.   
It is MUCH, MUCH higher.   
P5 >>> P6.

Do you see what happened here?   
Pressure that was marginally higher - resulted in performance that was much lower - and resulted in pain that was much much higher!

And the only difference?   
In scenario 2 - you had an option.

THAT is what life is about.

In life - if you feel stuck, you will be FAR MORE miserable for the exact same conditions, than if you have options.

It is true, not just for work.   
It is true for relationships. It is true for money. It is true for work.   
True for life.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2b3O!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F19f85c22-3170-4b4a-a9f2-34c8658d15a3_1024x1024.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2b3O!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F19f85c22-3170-4b4a-a9f2-34c8658d15a3_1024x1024.jpeg)

​

Spend time creating options.   
YOU ARE NOT STUCK.   
Even if the option you create is only marginally better, it will compound subconsciously to make you feel more powerful eventually.

**The worst enemy in your life is the person who convinces you that you do not have any other choice. That you are stuck. That you do not have options.   
Often that person is you yourself.**
