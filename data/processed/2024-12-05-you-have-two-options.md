# ​ Why do you want to prolong this pain?

I got an email yesterday:   
Arranged marriage in 2023.   
Everything happened quickly.   
Ignored a few red flags, thinking that things would be fine after marriage.   
Soon after the marriage, every day was a living hell.   
No respect.   
Asked to transfer full salary to mother-in-law.   
Now, getting divorced. *​  
I am just not able to deal with this divorce.* *Wherever I am, my mind keeps going back to him. My mind always keeps thinking about what he must be doing.*

I asked her: *​  
Do you want me to listen to you without judgement?* *​  
Or do you want my advice on what you should do?*

*I'll go for the 2nd option*, she replied.

Here is what I told her: *​  
Your husband hurt you once.* *​  
You are hurting yourself multiple times over and over.*

*What happened is unfortunate. It is clearly not desirable. You suffered.* *​  
Why do you want to prolong the suffering?* *​  
Here is why (and this will hurt and sound harsh, but is my truth)* *​  
Because it makes you feel like the victim.* *​  
It legitimizes your pain.* *​  
It allows you to wallow in your misery and tell yourself, "I suffered, so I am valid to feel this pain."*

*You have a wonderful life in front of you, <her name>.* *​  
You can wipe your tears, heal from the pain, and move on.* *​  
Or you can continue to suffer every single day, thinking and ruminating about the past.*

*The choice is for you to make.* *​  
But you can't make the choice until you truly understand that your current pain is your choice.* *​  
It is not imposed on you.* *​  
What happened to you was real.* *What you are making yourself go through is self-inflicted.*

*YOU MAKE THE CHOICE TO BE IN PAIN TODAY.* *​  
And until you choose not to be in this pain, you will never gather the courage to move on.*

## 1 new thing I learnt this week

India holds (as of Sep 2024) approx 510 tonnes of Gold in India (about 300 tonnes belonging to India is stored outside of India).   
Within India, the gold is stored in high-security vaults of RBI in Mumbai and Nagpur.

The RBI treats information about these storage facilities as highly confidential to maintain the security of the nation's gold reserves. As such, photographs or detailed descriptions of these vaults are not made available to the public.

Imagine - 500 tonnes of gold. Insane!
