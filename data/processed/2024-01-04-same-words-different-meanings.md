**​  
Light vs Dark**

One day, my wife, Ruchi, applied a light pink lipstick.   
Our then-4-year-old daughter, Uzma said, “Mummy, please apply a light lipstick.”  
Ruchi told her that it was a light shade.  
Uzma replied, “No, this is dark. Please apply a light one.”  
​

This argument went on and on.  
Until it struck us.  
For Uzma, light meant brightness and dark meant darkness.  
So light lipstick meant a BRIGHT lipstick.  
And dark lipstick meant a dim colour.  
​

Same words, different meanings!

And I realised, that's what happens to us in life!

We listen to the words of others, assuming the meaning that we attach to them.   
We listen to the words of others, without trying to understand what the words mean to them.  
We listen to the words of others, to form an impression that we believe in. Not the meaning they wish to communicate.   
​

Same words, different meanings.
