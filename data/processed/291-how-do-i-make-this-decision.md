# This job or that?

I received an email yesterday:  
  
*I’m currently working at Company X <edited>, and while the role has given me strong exposure, the night-shift schedule has started to affect my health.   
  
With a newborn child (3 months old) and growing family responsibilities, improving my physical and mental well-being has become a priority, alongside ensuring long-term financial stability for my family.*

***I have an offer from Company Y <edited> that presents a different set of trade-offs.**   
  
On the positive side, it moves me away from night shifts, places me in a high-scale, problem-heavy environment, and pushes me into an uncomfortable zone where I believe meaningful learning and growth happen.* 

*At the same time, this decision comes with real considerations.   
  
1. Relocating to Mumbai would mean starting afresh away from my parents and immediate family support.   
  
2. My wife is currently working on her thesis, which she plans to submit by mid-April, and I’m actively supporting her through this phase. A relocation would require careful timing and coordination to ensure this transition doesn’t disrupt her academic commitments.   
  
3. Financially, the move would also reduce my short-term savings due to higher living costs.* 

*I’d really value your perspective on how to think about such decisions.*

This is a classic ‘this job or that job’ conundrum.   
However, in my experience, the confusion is self-imposed.   
To figure if that was the case, I asked:   
*Did you apply for the Company Y role, or did it come your way?*

He replied:  
*I was looking for a change. It came my way.*

This instantly told me where the potential problem could be.

Here was my exact reply:  
*So that’s the issue. Something came your way, and now you are disturbed whether you should take it or not.   
  
But what’s happened here is that the conditions of this role aren’t ideal.   
If you had to craft the ideal role, this may not be it.* 

*It would perhaps be a similar role in a city that allows you to save more money, gives similar growth opportunities while giving you the time and space to manage your personal priorities.* 

*If you can find this role, you can find any other role.   
So go and chase the role you want, where this conflict wouldn’t be a reality.* 

***Don’t always take the first thing that comes your way.   
Use it as a sign to get the thing that you want.***   
*[(Share on X)](https://ctt.ac/UmJTG)*

This is true not just for jobs, but for everything else in life as well.   
The right choice to make is between options that YOU have chosen for yourself.   
Not options that have been given to you, without your permission or preference.

## 1 new thing I learnt this week

Our brains “taste” food before our mouth does.   
When you see or smell good food, your brain predicts what’s coming. It activates the saliva system in your brainstem.   
Because saliva is not just water. It is a digestive tool.

So when your brain expects food, it sends a signal to prime the whole system.
