**Questions define everything!**

So, a very interesting thing happened last week.  
I create a lot of content on LinkedIn. It's one of my top platforms that I really like creating content for.

As part of our weekly content plan every week, we conduct a poll.   
And because I have a fair share of people who are following me on LinkedIn, these polls usually get about 35-40,000 votes.  
Which is awesome, right?  
Imagine 35-40,000 people telling you which of those options they believe in.

These questions have no logic, frankly.  
They're just driven by my curiosity.  
They're just driven by, "Oh, I wonder what people think of X? or Y?"   
And why do people do X? Or Y?

So last week, the question that I asked was: Do you believe in ghosts or spirits?

And it was crazy how that sparked an outrage of sorts.   
"OMG, is this a question to be asked on LinkedIn?"  
"Is this what we've come to?"  
"Have you run out of content?"  
"Look at Ankur Warikoo doing a BeerBiceps."  
It was amusing!

​

​

By this time, I am used to such comments coming my way.   
But what fascinated me this time and set me wondering was WHY would asking a question, even if it's the stupidest question on Earth, be ever questioned?  
​**Why would asking a question ever be questioned?   
​**[(Tweet this)](https://ctt.ac/6dHUw)​

And here is my reason why.  
Recall the time when we asked a question in class and our teacher dismissed it as a stupid question?

Suddenly we felt alone.   
The entire class laughed at us.   
We felt the teacher doesn’t like us anymore.   
We wondered why did we even ask the question in the first place.   
The best thing would have been to shut up instead.

So that’s what we did.   
We stopped asking.   
We stopped questioning.   
Instead, we focused on memorising answers.   
Answers that helped us score in exams.

So today, as adults, we have trained ourselves not to ask.   
Just to answer.

What we do not realise is that the only way to grow in life is by asking questions.   
Is to never ever stop your curiosity.   
Especially if we stop asking questions because of what we think people might think.

That's the way I have grown.   
And I love this way of growing :))

So I implore upon you, request you for two things.

One, never dismiss anybody for asking a question.   
Especially if you're a parent.  
Never dismiss your kids when they ask you a question, no matter how stupid that question is, irritable that question is, or how often that question is repeated.  
Don't kill their curiosity.  
​**Don't dismiss anybody when they ask a question.   
​**[(Tweet this)](https://ctt.ac/0kelK)​

And number two, ask questions.  
As stupid as it may sound when you ask them, as scary as it may sound when you ask them, as embarrassing as it may be when people mock you for asking that question, ask questions.  
Because the only one winning in that moment is you.  
​**When everybody is laughing at you for asking a question but you got the answer to that question - you are the one winning, not them.** [(Tweet this)](https://ctt.ac/ceebV)​  
Become that person.

# 📕 Book I'm reading this week

I started reading **[How to Win an Indian Election: What Political Parties Don't Want You to Know](https://amzn.to/45RRnxO)** by Shivam Shankar Singh.  
It is a gripping tale of what happens behind the scenes for every election - whether state or central and how politics is all about just one thing at the end of it all - how to win elections!   
It gets sad often when you recognise how everyone is played with and manipulated. But it is true, so you cannot run away from it as well.   
Read the book to be informed and to be aware.
