**Hope is everything**

May 1998. IIT Delhi Campus.  
​  
The results of the toughest exam in the world, IIT JEE, had been announced.  
Back then, the results were printed out on sheets of paper, and pasted on the walls of all IIT campuses.  
Papa and I rode on our Bajaj scooter, from Lajpat Nagar to the huge IIT Delhi Campus.  
​  
I had always dreamt of entering the campus as a student.  
And if my confidence was to be believed, I would be entering it as a student, soon.  
Very very soon.  
​  
There were about 200-odd kids and parents when we arrived.  
Holding their index finger against the paper stuck on the wall.  
​  
There were 7 such sheets. I managed to get to one such sheet of paper.  
My name was not there.  
Initially, I remember not feeling concerned at all, because I was so convinced I had made it.  
​  
I went through the second sheet.  
Third.  
Fourth.  
Fifth.  
The fear has started to crawl up now.  
Sixth.  
​  
The fear was now real.  
​  
The seventh sheet.  
No.  
It wasn't there.  
​  
I rubbed my eyes.  
I had good eyesight, but in such moments you will believe anything.  
​  
"My name is not there."  
"That can't be. Check again,” Papa said.  
​  
He still hadn't given up on the confidence that had disappeared within me.  
"It is not there, Papa. I did not make it."  
"Wait, let me check again."  
​  
I cried my entire way back.  
​  
There were no pillion-driver helmets back then.  
So Papa could see my tears through the rear view mirror.  
Maybe he was crying too.  
I couldn't see.  
​  
My life felt like it was over before it had even started.

​

How would I face Ma?  
Face my relatives?  
My friends, some of whom would have gotten through the exam?  
​  
​**There are fewer things heavier than the burden of letting your parents down.  
​**[(tweet this)](https://ctt.ac/Y2w4O)​  
​  
Ma wiped my tears.

​  
"Why did you want to go to IIT?"  
"Because I want to go to the US for my PhD."  
"And do only IITians go to the US for a PhD?"  
"Of course not."  
"Well, then you will be one of those!"  
​  
She gave me hope.  
We made it! :)  
​  
​**Sometimes, all we need in life is for someone to believe in us, more than we believe in ourselves.

**Results of last week's survey**

Do you live with your parents?

1. Same city, same house
2. Same city, different house
3. Different city
4. Different country
5. My parents are no more

Here are the results:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UFBj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8211061e-ade7-486f-878e-54359746fb96_889x207.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UFBj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8211061e-ade7-486f-878e-54359746fb96_889x207.png)

​

1. Not surprisingly, until 22, most of you stay with your parents. From 18 onwards, you can start seeing the shift to a different city (most likely for college).
2. From 23 onwards, there is a massive shift to different cities. It becomes dominant for those beyond 30.
3. 30s is also the time when parents start leaving us. Notice the last row of "my parents are no more".
4. What is encouraging then is the upward trend of "same city, different house" - it retains one's independence while ensuring you are close to your ageing parents.

**My response:**​  
I stay in the same city but in a different house from both my parents and in-laws. It is such a blessing that I wouldn't have it any other way today.
