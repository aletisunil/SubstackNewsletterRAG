# This is not your fault

*“I want to reach 1 crore in the next 3-4 years.”*  
I received an email this morning.  
  
This was written by someone who earns well, has a stable job, is well-qualified, and has been investing for over 5 years.  
Her investment corpus is 25 lakhs, which is impressive.  
But her email mentioned a common, naive goal: to get rich quick.  
  
In personal finance, compounding is key.  
It is possible you already know about it.   
  
But this email isn’t about compounding.   
  
It’s about a problem with the youth:   
You understand compounding, but haven’t experienced it.  
That’s not your fault.  
  
Because you haven’t had what is the most important ingredient of compounding:  
Time!  
  
If you’re in your 20s or 30s, you haven’t seen 15-20 years of compounding.  
You know what it means. But you haven’t seen it.   
This leads to a desire to “Get rich fast,” like in the email.  
  
The truth is — The only quick way to wealth is the lottery.  
Nearly everything else takes time and relies on compounding.  
  
But without seeing it work, you most likely doubt it.   
And end up taking risky shortcuts or scams promising instant returns.  
  
Now, you have two options:  
  
1. Ignore compounding and chase quick riches  
2. Trust it and build wealth steadily.  
  
What will you choose?

## 1 new thing I learnt this week

Dravidian language scripts (such as Telugu, Kannada, Tamil, Malayalam, etc.) tend to be “curvy” because they were inscribed on banana and palm leaves in their early days.   
  
Because straight strokes (especially sharp vertical or horizontal ones) might tear or split the leaf, scribes may have preferred smoother, rounded strokes that put less stress on the material.
