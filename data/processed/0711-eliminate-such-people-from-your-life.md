# Not everyone is rational

One of my favourite scenes from the movie ‘The Dark Knight’ is where Alfred shares a story.   
  
*A long time ago, I was in Burma.  
My friends and I were working for the local government.   
They were trying to bribe the local leaders with precious stones.   
But their caravans were being raided in a forest by a bandit, who stole the precious stones.   
So we went looking for the stones.   
But in six months, we never found anyone who bought them or sold them.   
One day, I saw a child playing with one of the stolen ruby.   
The bandit had been throwing the precious stones away after stealing them.*

He then goes on to say:  
*Because some men aren’t looking for anything logical, like money.   
They can’t be bought, bullied, reasoned, or negotiated with.   
Some men just want to watch the world burn.*

While in this movie, he was referring to the villain (The Joker), I love this quote because it is honestly the most important lesson in life.

This world has rational and irrational people.   
We may think that most people are rational.   
But it is the irrational ones, the ones who cannot be bought, bullied, reasoned or negotiated with, that create havoc.

They do not serve any purpose, but they stall all progress.   
They do not gain from any defeat, but they ensure there is no victory.   
They do not believe in the truth, but they will ensure the truth never prevails.

We all have dealt with such people.   
It could be our parents, our friends, our bosses, or our colleagues.

When you find such a person in your life, the only thing you should do is disengage.   
  
You can never win against such a person.   
You can only preserve your sanity.   
Disengage.

If it’s family, move out.   
If it’s friends, cut them out.   
If it’s your boss/colleague, find another job.

**You will NEVER make peace with someone who never desires it in the first place.**

# My most exciting project ever!

For the past 6 months, a small team of 7 has been working silently on what is my most exciting project till date.   
  
We call it **IGC - [India Genius Challenge](http://www.indiageniuschallenge.com).**

IGC is a nation-wide search for India’s brightest young minds aged 13-15 years.

For a long time, genius in our culture has been limited to academic excellence.   
But we all know that true geniuses are far beyond that.  
They are aware of the world, they think logically and critically, and are able to communicate effectively.   
IGC attempts to find such geniuses at a young age.

IGC will have 2 rounds: an online round (absolutely free) where kids will play daily challenges around General Knowledge, Critical Thinking and Logical Reasoning.

They will win daily, weekly, and monthly prizes ranging from books and certificates, to iPads and iPhones.

Share this in your network and encourage kids aged 13-15 to play the challenge.  
The site is now open for registration.   
Visit **[India Genius Challenge](http://www.indiageniuschallenge.com).**

This online round will lead to the national round in Delhi, where the brightest students will compete to win a scholarship worth Rs. 10L.
