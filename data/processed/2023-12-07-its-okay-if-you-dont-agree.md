**​  
Unshakeable beliefs**

Peter Thiel, in his wonderful book 'Zero to One,' describes one of his favourite interview questions:  
"What is one personal belief that very few people agree with you on?"

I love this question.   
In an instant, it shows me the strength of the individual's conviction and the comfort of holding a belief that most of the world doesn't align with.

Here are my responses:

* Empathy cannot be taught. You are either born with it or not.
* If you get angry, you still have a long way to go.
* If you can't write it down, you have no idea what you are talking about.
* Focus beats intelligence and hard work.
* If you learn something and tell people about it - you are not learning. You are simply showing off.

Would love to know yours.

# ​ 📕 Book I am reading right now

Started reading **[NOISE: A Flaw in Human Judgement](https://amzn.to/47Jo4Oa)** by Daniel Kahneman (author of 'Thinking Fast and Slow').   
Loving the book. It explains how and why humans are so susceptible to noise and bias in decision-making.   
We all make bad judgements more than we think.   
The book explores what we can do to make better ones.
