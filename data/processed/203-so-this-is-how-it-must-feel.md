# Loss of control is very unsettling

We are in Egypt this week, visiting the ruins of a civilization nearly 4,000 years old.   
As a family, we love history - and Egypt is history on steroids, haha!

As a country, this place reminds me of India.   
People are still largely poor.   
There is a lot of construction all around.   
The society is vastly different from western civilization - the culture goes back centuries, and the ways are very unique to the place.

And, much like India, it is a culture of constant negotiation.   
EVERYTHING is up for negotiation.   
The price of the hotel, the tour guide fee, the price of a memento, and even how much tip to give.   
Everyone is constantly selling you something.   
On the streets, along the footpath, in the hotel, at the historic sites.

We are so used to it in India.   
Haggling with auto drivers, shopkeepers, service providers, and everyone else.   
The smaller the city, the more negotiable the price is.   
And that is largely because the original price is not a known price.   
That’s okay for us in India, because we know the price - more or less.   
But for a non-local, they have no idea how much does a replica of the Taj Mahal cost, how much an auto ride from Sarojini Nagar to CP costs, or how much to tip someone who helps you with luggage at the airport.

That’s exactly what we are feeling right now.   
We don’t know the price.   
So we are constantly thinking - are we being fleeced? Are we being taken for a ride?   
Thankfully, money is not so much of a concern. But even then, this idea of not being in control is unsettling.

That is so true for life in general.

Life is surprisingly better if we know in advance what to expect.   
EVEN if the thing to expect is not desirable.   
What disturbs us is the unpredictable.   
Where we do not know.

**Much of life is not so much about controlling the outcome.   
But to be able to predict it.
